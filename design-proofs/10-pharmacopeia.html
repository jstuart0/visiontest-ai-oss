VISIONTEST.AI · MONOGRAPH OF VISUAL REGRESSION ASSAY · CLASS II / NON-DESTRUCTIVE

Visual Regression Pharmacopoeia
for Interactive Surfaces

A reference method for the determination of pixel-congruence, perceptual drift, and semantic equivalence across multi-platform user interfaces.

This monograph prescribes the standard procedure by which the VisionTest.ai assay apparatus shall capture, compare, and adjudicate rendered specimens of an application under test across the supported matrix of web · iOS · android runtimes. The assay combines a pixel-level congruence test (Δpx), a structural similarity index (SSIM), a learned perceptual metric (LPIPS) and a semantic embedding distance (DINOv2) to yield a composite Visual Equivalence Quotient (VEQ). Specimens exceeding the tolerance band defined in §4.2 are routed to the adjudication queue (§5). Flaky specimens — those whose intra-run variance exceeds σ² = 0.0025 — shall be placed under quarantine pending root-cause analysis per §6. Autonomous remediation (§7) is permitted only where the candidate patch passes all three convergence criteria enumerated therein.

Caution · Read Before Use

This method is non-destructive but may consume significant compute. Do not run against production surfaces without an approved X-VT-ASSAY-MODE: observe header. Specimens obtained under unstable network (RTT > 180 ms) are inadmissible (§6.3).

§1.1 · Method Summary per VT-RGX-0001.A

A suite of reference specimens is captured from the application under test on a warm, stable runner. For each candidate build, a matched specimen set is produced under the identical matrix (browser × device × viewport × locale × theme). The specimen pair is submitted to the composite VEQ pipeline. A specimen is declared in equivalence when all four metrics fall within the tolerance band. Any exceedance is escalated per §4.2 and adjudicated per §5.

§3.2 · Findingsper examiner · 2026-04-23 09:22 Z

Observation A · A sustained chromatic shift is recorded within Region of Interest ROI·1 (upper banner, coordinates [x=420..1500, y=72..180]). The hue channel (H) reports a mean deviation of +3.218° with a σ of 0.084°, consistent with a palette token migration from --accent-500 (#27436B) to --accent-600 (#2E5A94). The shift is reproducible across n = 9 consecutive captures.

Observation B · ROI·2 (primary action button, coordinates [x=420..612, y=684..804]) exhibits both a hue drift of +2.04° and a ~6 px vertical displacement of the descender baseline. SSIM over this region falls to 0.941 (reference 0.998), placing this region below the lower acceptance bound.

Observation C · ROI·3 (left sidebar, coordinates [x=72..344, y=132..984]) shows a uniform luminance shift of −2.8 %. The effect is consistent with a token change from --rule-100 to --rule-200; however the magnitude remains inside SSIM tolerance.

Interpretation · The candidate specimen shows evidence of an intentional design-token revision (accent hue, rule weight). No layout-structural regressions were detected — the DINOv2 embedding distance (d = 0.1142) is within semantic tolerance. The failure is tolerance-band pixel rather than structural.

Assessment · The examiner recommends that a baseline refresh be performed on the affected components (BillingCard, Sidebar.Nav, Button.Primary) pending design-review sign-off. Until such refresh is lodged, the candidate specimen remains in rejected status and MUST NOT be promoted to the stable channel (§4.2.c).

Recommendation · Submit CR VT-CR-0148 to the design authority referencing design-token-migration/v2.0.0. Upon approval, trigger POST /api/v1/baselines/refresh?scope=billing. The autonomous-fix subsystem (§7) has proposed a candidate patch (see §7.1) that would not close this finding because the observed drift is design-intentional, not defective.
